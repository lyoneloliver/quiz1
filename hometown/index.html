Surabaya is the capital of East Java and Indonesia's second-largest city after Jakarta, making it a key center for business, education, and industry. Nicknamed the "City of Heroes," it played a crucial role in Indonesia's independence movement, especially during the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, which became a symbol of national pride and bravery. Today, Surabaya is a bustling metropolis where history and modern development blend seamlessly, featuring a mix of colonial-era buildings, wide boulevards, and contemporary urban landscapes.

About My Hometown

My hometown is Surabaya, a place known for its friendly people, cultural heritage, and beautiful natural scenery. It's a city that blends tradition with modern life, making it both comfortable to live in and exciting to explore.

The city is also known for its vibrant energy and strong sense of community. As one of the country's economic powerhouses, Surabaya is home to major shipping ports and industries, earning it a reputation as the gateway to eastern Indonesia. Despite its rapid growth, the city maintains a welcoming atmosphere, with locals often noted for their straightforward and friendly character. Surabaya's culture reflects a rich Javanese heritage, mixed with influences from Chinese, Arab, and European settlers who have shaped its identity over centuries.
