Welcome to Surabaya!

The vibrant capital of East Java, is more than just a bustling city; it’s a place where history, culture, and modern life blend seamlessly. Known as the “City of Heroes,” Surabaya offers a rich tapestry of experiences for locals and visitors alike. From its lively markets to its iconic landmarks, every corner tells a story.

Bromo

Tengger Caldera, East Java

Learn More

Mount Bromo is an active volcano and part of the Tengger massif, famous for its stunning sunrise views and dramatic landscapes. Visitors often enjoy trekking and jeep tours across the caldera.

House of Sampoerna

Surabaya, East Java

Learn More

House of Sampoerna is a historic museum in Surabaya showcasing the history of Indonesia's famous kretek cigarette industry. Visitors can explore the museum and watch the traditional cigarette rolling process.

Mangrove Forest

Mangrove area

Learn More

The Mangrove Forest area offers a serene environment with rich biodiversity, perfect for nature walks, bird watching, and learning about the importance of mangroves in coastal protection.

Kenjeran Beach

Surabaya, East Java

Learn More

Kenjeran Beach offers a relaxing seaside experience with local food vendors, traditional fishing boats, and a great spot to enjoy the sunset.

Surabaya Monument

Surabaya, East Java

Learn More

The Sura and Baya Monument, an iconic landmark of Surabaya, features statues of a shark (Sura) and a crocodile (Baya) locked in combat. The monument symbolizes the origin of the city's name and represents the courage and fighting spirit of Surabaya’s people. It’s a popular spot for photos and relaxing around the area.
